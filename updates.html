Update #1

Date: Jan 9th 2021

As those of you on Cloudy Nights might know, this month's astroimaging challenge is of M 42, or the Orion Nebula. As I had wanted to get a stack longer than 5 min, I got more data.I used just the new data, which ended up being ~30 min, which was split between 1.6 sec and 2 sec and f2.8-f4. I struggled with postprocessing some, but thought I'd post my final image so far.

I used Levels, Saturation, and Contrast a lot in GIMP to process this image. GIMP is free and about as easy to use as Photoshop was for me (not too easy, but not real bad). I'm running GIMP on a Chromebook, and stacking images with Deep Sky Stacker, which is Windows-only and free on a Windows machine. All astroimages that aren't otherwise stated are copyright CN user Leia 2021- Please don't steal my images.
